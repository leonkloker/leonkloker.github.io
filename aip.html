I had the great opportunity to conduct research together with scientist from the Quantum Performance Laboratory at Sandia. This group's mission is to develop and deploy techniques for assessing the performance of quantum computing hard- and software.
My project revolved around the evaluation of different quantum circuits that all execute the same algorithm. The choice of quantum circuit can greatly influence the probability that the algorithm fails due to noise and decoherence. The core idea was to develop a Graph Neural Network which takes a quantum circuit as an input and predicts the probability that the given single-outcome circuit executes successfully on a quantum computer. The employed Graph Attention models were then compared to an existing Convolutional Network tackling the same task.
For me, the project was a great opportunity to learn about the basics of quantum circuits and to get hands-on experience with Graph Neural Networks.
