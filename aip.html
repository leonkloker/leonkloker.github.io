While working as a Graduate Research Assistant at the Institute for Modeling Hydraulic and Environmental Systems at the University of Stuttgart, I published my research findings in the journal Physics of Fluids. The co-author of this paper is Carina Bringedal, who advised me during my bachelor thesis and continued helping me throughout the research process. The paper revolves around the stability of saline groundwater flow that is caused by evaporation at the surface. The dynamics of the system is governed by a system of partial differential equations, which allow for a time-dependent ground state solution. Criteria for the existence and growth of any perturbation from this ground state were derived via linear stability theory. The stability criteria as well as the predicted convection patterns were then compared to numerical simulations of the system using self-written finite volume code.
This kind of research can explain the formation of salt lakes and erosion in arid regions and is thus of great importance for the understanding of agricultural and environmental systems.
