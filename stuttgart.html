Research Assistant

  • Organization:
    Institute of Thermodynamics and Thermal Process Engineering
    Institute of Applied Analysis and Numerical Simulation
  • Location: Stuttgart
  • Duration: April 2019 - December 2020
  • Skills: Monte-Carlo simulation, Statistical Physics, Model reduction

I worked as a Research Assistant during 3 semesters of my undergraduate education at the University of Stuttgart. I was involved in two projects, one at the Institute of Thermodynamics and Thermal Process Engineering and one at the Institute of Applied Analysis and Numerical Simulation. The first project was about the development of a novel force field that can be used in Monte-Carlo simulation of primary alcohols mixed with carbon-dioxide for the prediction of the thermodynamic properties of fluids (link). Here, understanding the simulation of different thermodynamic ensembles as well as the underlying physics behind the statistical behaviour of the molecules was paramount.
The second project concerned the implementation of the discrete empirical interpolation method in order to efficiently solve parameterized partial differential equations (link). The method's computation speed-up to accuracy trade-off was investigated by applying it to a parameterized heat equation.
