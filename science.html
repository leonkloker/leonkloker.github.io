  • Title: Language Modeling Materializes a World Model of Protein Biology

About the publication

This work investigates how protein language models — large transformers trained with a masked-token objective on amino acid sequences — implicitly learn a representation of protein biology far beyond next-token prediction. By analyzing the ESM family of models (including ESM2 and ESMC) across scales and depths, we show that structural and biophysical properties of proteins emerge inside the network as a smooth function of compute, parameter count, and transformer layer depth. The figure illustrates the masked-language-modeling setup (A), compute-optimal scaling behavior of emergent protein structure (B), the gap between ESM2 and ESMC at matched model scale (C), and the layerwise trajectory along which structural and functional information materializes as representations propagate through the network (D). Together, these results argue that language modeling on protein sequences alone is sufficient to materialize a coherent internal "world model" of protein biology, providing a quantitative framework for understanding when and where biological structure appears inside foundation models for proteins.
