Graduate Research Assistant

  • Organization: Lab for Integrative Imaging & Molecular Diagnostics.
  • Location: Palo Alto
  • Duration: January - June, 2023
  • Skills: Computer Vision, Python, Statistical Analysis

I was a member of the Machine Learning research team at Ansys. This team is focused on investigating applications of Machine Learning with high business impact. These possibilities can range from fundamental AI research, over speeding up simulations using surrogate models, to building an own "Github Copilot" for automated geometry generation. During my summer internship, I worked on Neural Operator Learning, a novel approach to solve partial differential equations using deep learning. Here, unlike physics-informed Neural Networks, the model does not just learn one instance of a PDE but the solution operator between function spaces. One example would be to learn the mapping of an initial condition to the solution of the PDE at a given time and certain query points. Specifically, I developed new Transformer-based models to learn the solution operator of the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations in 2 dimensions. Depending on the complexity of the dataset, the Transformer models yielded smaller relative errors than previous SOTA models such as the Fourier Neural Operator . Overall, I had a great time learning about the inner-workings of Transformers and their applications to PDEs as well as how to build up a ML project from scratch with PyTorch.
