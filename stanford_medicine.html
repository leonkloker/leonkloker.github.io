Graduate Research Assistant

  • Organization: Lab for Integrative Imaging and Molecular Diagnostics
  • Location: Palo Alto
  • Duration: January - June, 2023
  • Skills: Computer Vision, Python, Statistical Analysis

I worked as a Graduate Research Assistant in Professor Ruijiang Li's lab at Stanford's Palo Alto medicine campus. During my first quarter there, I worked on automated cell-segmentation and classification on the single-cell level in digitalized H&E images. This image modality is common practice in pathology in order to determine the presence of cancerous cells. Leveraging ML algorithms to automate the analysis allows for a more comprehensive picture of the tumor microenvironment, which is a key factor in determining the prognosis of a patient.
In my second quarter there, I switched from ML algorithm development to the statistical analysis of the arising spatial cell patterns once the H&E image is segmented and cells classified. The main focus of this part of the pipeline was to find spatial biomarkers that are predictive of overall survival of a patient after receiving a certain type of Immunotherapy. This was done by looking at Kaplan-Meier survival curves of several biomarkers as well as their corresponding hazard ratios obtained by Cox regression.
The goal of this research is to enable oncologists to make more informed treatment decisions based on the expressions of spatial biomarkers that are indicative of treatment response.
